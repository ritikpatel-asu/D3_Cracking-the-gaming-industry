Cracking the Gaming Industry

Why should we analyze the video game industry?

This project explores the video game industry by analyzing various datasets related to game sales, genres and platforms. We aim to uncover trends and insights into how different genres have evolved over time and the impact of publishers on the gaming market. Through interactive visualizations, we will tell the story of video game popularity and sales distribution across different platforms. By examining historical data, we can identify patterns in consumer preferences, the rise and fall of specific genres, and the influence of major publishers. This analysis not only highlights the dynamics of the gaming industry but also provides valuable insights for developers and marketers looking to understand market trends and consumer behavior.

Correlation between Publisher and Games

This visualization provides an interactive heatmap that highlights the percentage of games released by publishers within specific genres. Each cell represents a publisher-genre pair, with the color intensity indicating the proportion of games released. Upon selecting two cells, a detailed comparison graph is generated. This graph displays key metrics such as the total number of games published, genre-specific releases, overall sales, and regional sales across North America, Europe, and Japan. The mirrored bar chart format allows users to easily compare these metrics side by side, offering valuable insights into the performance and market reach of different publishers in various genres.

We can see that Nintendo focused on platform and puzzle genres as seen in the heatmap, which is a family-friendly genre, and parents are also not hesitant in buying these games for their kids. Similarly, Electronic Arts mainly focused on the sports genre, which helped them increase their sales because sports lovers would play sports games such as Cricket07, FIFA, etc. Moreover, the analysis reveals that consoles like PlayStation and Xbox have diversified their game offerings, catering to a broader audience by including various genres such as action, adventure, and role-playing games. This strategic approach has allowed them to maintain a competitive edge in the market, appealing to both casual and hardcore gamers alike. Hence, it can be said that sticking to certain types of game genres for a long time can help the publisher acquire a top position in the gaming industry.
